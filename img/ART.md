Overall look: high-fidelity 2D animation with crisp vector-like linework and bold, readable silhouettes. Use clean outlines in a dark navy or near-black (#0B1C3F–#1A1A1A), with subtle line-weight variation (about 0.3% of canvas width, thicker on outer contours, thinner on interior details). Color is bright, saturated, and friendly; avoid muddy mixes and heavy textures. Favor flat fills with two-step cel shading (one shadow, one highlight), soft-edged shadows at 10–20% opacity, and small, punchy specular highlights. Keep backgrounds simple and graphic with large, clear shapes, gentle parallax, and desaturated variants of the character palette for separation. Use squash-and-stretch, smears (2–3 ghosted duplicates or elongated shapes), and lively overshoot/settle to sell motion. Impact frames can momentarily shift palette (hot pinks, yellows, cyans like #FF4FC3, #FFD600, #40C4FF) or invert line color for snap, but return immediately to the core palette (e.g., lemon #FFEB3B, tangerine #FF9800, cherry #FF5252, sky #40C4FF, lime #AEEA00, violet #7C4DFF). Keep the overall mood bright and playful; prioritize clarity and comedic timing over realism.
